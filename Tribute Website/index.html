MITCH LUCKER

Mitch's childhood & Early Life

Mitch Lucker was born in Riverside on October 20, 1984, and lived there for 15 years in a middle-class family, demonstrating his passion for music at an early age. His father's name was Kip Lucker and his mother Denise Haddad. He also had an older brother and a younger sister, Cliff and Heather. According to his parents, Lucker was not interested in playing the guitar or any instrument, but he did seem interested in being in a band, as a vocalist.

In an interview with The AU Interview, he said the bands that influenced him to form a band were Korn, Deftones, Slayer, Sepultura. Everything my dad would buy and bring home to me and my brother saying "Hey, listen to this".

His Death

Mitch Lucker died on November 1, 2012; he was 28 years old.9 The Orange County Coroner's Office announced that he had suffered serious injuries due to a motorcycle accident in Huntington Beach, California; he was riding a 2013 Harley Davidson. A report stated that Lucker crashed his motorcycle shortly after 9p.m. on October 31.
His motorcycle continued on its path down the street and struck a Dakota pick-up with people, who were not seriously injured. Lucker was immediately transported to the ICU at the Medical Center where he died at exactly 6:17 a.m.

Musical Carrer

Following the formation of Suicide Silence in 2002, the band played their first show at a local area in Riverside and at that time the lineup consisted of members Chris Garza and Rick Ash on guitar, Mike Bodkins on bass, Josh Goddard on drums and two vocalists, Lucker and Tanner Womack.

After Womack's dismissal, Lucker remained as the band's sole vocalist, releasing their first demo the following year.

Suicide Silence's debut album, titled The Cleansing and released on September 18, 2007, was one of Century Media's best-selling releases with 7,250 copies sold in the first week. The album debuted at number 94 on the Billboard 200. The success of their first album earned them an invitation to perform at the Mayhem Festival in 2008. After this, the band toured Europe and the United States.

Their second album No Time to Bleed was released on June 30, 2009. The album reached number 32 on the Billboard 200 chart. The album sold 14 thousand copies in the first week of release in the United States alone.

The band's last album featuring Lucker, The Black Crown was released on July 12, 2011. By this time, their lyrics had begun to lose the previous controversial anti-religious content. When asked by Kerrang!, Lucker explained:

"I'm not trying to demean people's beliefs, it's about me and my life - this is my head open and poured out on paper! I still have the same beliefs and the same opinions, but I'm more open to everything. At this point in my life, I don't see the good in making people hate you for something you say. This record is for everyone."
